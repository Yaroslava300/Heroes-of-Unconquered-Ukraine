Vasyl Stus

(1938-1985)

Vasyl Stus is a Ukrainian poet, dissident, translator and human rights activist. He actively opposed the soviet regime, criticizing restrictions on freedom of speech and the national oppression of Ukrainians. His work is permeated with motifs of struggle, suffering and love for Ukraine. In 1972, he was arrested for "anti-soviet activities" and sentenced to camps. Despite the harsh conditions, he continued to write poems, many of which were destroyed by the camp administration. In 1980, he was convicted again, and in 1985 he died in the VS-389/36-1 camp in the perm region. Stus became a symbol of the indomitability of the Ukrainian spirit
