Oleksandr Dovzhenko

(1894-1956)

Oleksandr Dovzhenko is a prominent Ukrainian film director, writer, and screenwriter, one of the founders of world cinema. His films, such as "Zvenygora", "Arsenal", "Earth", became classics of the Ukrainian avant-garde. "Earth" is considered one of the best films in the history of cinema. Dovzhenko also worked as a writer, his works are marked by deep philosophy and patriotism. Despite world recognition, the soviet authorities repeatedly limited his work, forcing him to make propaganda films. He dreamed of creating free Ukrainian cinema, but worked under the strict control of the USSR. He died in 1956, leaving behind a huge cultural legacy
