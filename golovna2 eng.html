18-19th century

Skovoroda Kotlyarevsky Shevchenko

In the 18th century, the Ukrainian lands were finally divided between the russian and Austrian empires. The russian government eliminated the remnants of the autonomy of Left-Bank Ukraine and introduced serfdom. At the same time, in the Austrian Empire, the Western Ukrainian lands received certain reforms that contributed to the development of education and culture. In the 19th century, a national movement began aimed at the revival of the Ukrainian language, culture, and identity. Ivan Kotlyarevsky, Taras Shevchenko, and Panteleimon Kulish made a great contribution to this process. In 1846-1847, the Cyril and Methodius Brotherhood, the first Ukrainian political organization, operated. At the end of the century, the first Ukrainian political party, the RURP (Russo-Ukrainian Radical Party), was formed. Despite severe oppression, the Ukrainian national movement gradually gained strength, preparing the ground for the further struggle for independence
