Roman Shukhevych

(1907-1950)

Roman Shukhevych is a Ukrainian military figure, a general-coronet of the UPA (Ukrainian Insurgent Army), commander-in-chief of the UPA from 1943 to 1950. He led the struggle of Ukrainians against the soviet and nazi occupation. Under his leadership, the UPA conducted operations against the NKVD, the red army, and Polish military formations. Shukhevych evaded arrest for more than 7 years, operating underground. soviet troops surrounded the house in which Shukhevych was staying. Inside, a brief but fierce gunfight took place. According to the official version of the soviet secret services, he was shot during the battle. According to other versions, in order to avoid being captured, he shot himself. He became a legend of the Ukrainian liberation movement, a symbol of the struggle for Ukraine's independence
