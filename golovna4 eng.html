1921-1991

Konovalets Dovzhenko Shukhevych Bandera

The period from 1921 to 1991 in the history of Ukraine was extremely complex and controversial. In 1922, Ukraine became part of the soviet union, losing the independence gained during the years of the liberation struggle. In 1932-1933, the Ukrainian people experienced the Holodomor - an artificial famine organized by the Soviet authorities, which claimed millions of lives. The repressions of the 1930s destroyed the Ukrainian intelligentsia, clergy and national elite. During World War II, Ukraine became one of the main theaters of hostilities, and after its end it underwent harsh sovietization. In the post-war decades, industry developed, but at the same time national and cultural life was suppressed. In the 1960s-1980s, the dissident movement intensified, among which were Vasyl Stus, Vyacheslav Chornovil and Levko Lukyanenko. An important role in the struggle for independence was played by the national revival of the late 1980s, which led to the declaration of independence of Ukraine on August 24, 1991
