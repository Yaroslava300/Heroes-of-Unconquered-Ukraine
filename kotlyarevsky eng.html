Ivan Kotlyarevsky

(1769-1838)

Ivan Kotlyarevsky is the founder of new Ukrainian literature, the author of the famous poem "Aeneid", which was the first major literary work written in the living Ukrainian language. In his "Aeneid", he skillfully combined the ancient plot with Ukrainian folk traditions, humor and satire. Kotlyarevsky is also the author of the plays "Natalka Poltavka" and "Moskal-charivnyk", which laid the foundations of Ukrainian theater. His work became a powerful impetus for the development of Ukrainian culture
