17-18th century

Khmelnytsky Doroshenko

In the 17th and 18th centuries, Ukrainian lands were under the rule of the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth, the moscow Kingdom, and the Ottoman Empire. A significant event was the National Liberation War led by Bohdan Khmelnytsky (1648-1657), which led to the formation of the Cossack state - the Hetmanate. In 1654, Ukraine concluded the Pereyaslav Treaty with muscovy, which subsequently led to the loss of independence. In the second half of the 17th century, the Cossack state experienced the Ruin - a period of internal strife and division into Right-Bank and Left-Bank Ukraine. At the end of the century, moscow gradually limited the autonomy of the Hetmanate. In 1708-1709, Hetman Ivan Mazepa tried to free Ukraine from russian rule, but was defeated in the Battle of Poltava. In 1764, catherine II finally abolished the Hetmanate, and in 1775 the Zaporozhian Sich was destroyed. This marked the end of the autonomous Cossack system in Ukrainian lands
