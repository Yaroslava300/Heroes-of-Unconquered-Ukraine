Hryhoriy Skovoroda

(1722–1794)

Hryhoriy Skovoroda is an outstanding Ukrainian philosopher, poet, teacher and traveler. He is called the "Ukrainian Socrates" for his teachings on happiness, morality and freedom. He abandoned a secular career, choosing the life of a wandering philosopher. His works, in particular fables and philosophical treatises, contain deep reflections on the meaning of life and human nature. Skovoroda believed in "related work" - that is, that each person should do what he is called to do. His ideas were ahead of their time and remain relevant today
